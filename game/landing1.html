Level 2

Objective

 Collect 5 memories to unlock a conversation with your parents about the current situation.

Statistics

    

    

    0 out of 5

Cutscene

HAPPY, COMFORTING MUSIC PLAYS

YOU ENTER A COZY, COMFORTABLE HOME. OLENA IS IN THE KITCHEN AND IVAN IS READING A PAPER AT THE DINING TABLE. THE HOME IS WELL-LIT AND SUNLIGHT STREAMS THROUGH THE WINDOW.

Hey, I’m home!

Hey, honey! You’re late. Long practice?

Yeah, they needed an extra run-through to make sure everything was working.

They really are dedicated, huh?

Hey, Dad. Yeah, well, it’s for marks, so we’re all trying to make it passable, at least.
Oh, c’mon. I didn’t raise a kid who wants ‘passable’.

I know, Doctor, I’ll do well.

Now, now, you two, settle down. Dinner’s going to be ready soon. Wash up then have a seat.

Interactions

This is me, just an average teenager. Mom didn’t need to tell me to wash up, though— I’ve learned something from COVID-19 over the past two years, at least.
You’re reading the news?

I was. Now I’m just doing the daily crossword puzzle.

What, nothing exciting to talk about in the news?

...

What?

We can chat about that later. Go talk to your mother.

This computer is near the end of its life, but once you enter university, your father will get you a new one. That’s his promise.
The posters on the wall are of a few American rappers. Honestly, you don’t remember their names, but your friends really like them, so you collect the posters too.
The closet looks packed full. It probably needs to be organized soon.

Don't turn that on now -- let's eat first.

Hey, Mom. Do you need any help?

No, sweetheart, I’ve done it all. Just grab this plate and set it on the table.

Sure! It smells great.

Thank you. Take a seat when you’re ready to eat.

So, any news?

About work? No. Nothing too exciting happened at the hospital.

Thank goodness. It’s been tense enough as it is.

Have you heard anything?
A couple of the women on the block and I were chatting at the store earlier today- I bought extra food because of it. Just in case.

Good. We should make bags just in case- keep them in the car.
That’s probably a good idea.

Wait, but like… the war isn’t actually going to happen, right?

No, honey, but it never pays to be unprepared.

That’s true.

Choices

What’s been happening lately?
Why are we worried?
We need to ‘prepare’?

What’s been happening lately?

Tensions between our Ukrainian government and Russia have been escalating. I didn’t think it would
impact us beyond sanctions originally, but they’re really starting to even bring fear into the world leaders.
A lot of countries were speaking to Russia to end the conflict, right?
Yes. Small skirmishes along the border have already begun.
But like, we’re in the middle of town. They’re not going to attack normal people, right?
They can’t. That’s a war crime, technically.
Okay… you’re so calm about it.
With the whole world watching, what do we need to worry about?

Why are we worried?

Well, it will definitely impact daily life, even if it’s just a little.
Yeah. Some of your older classmates might enlist soon, so you won’t really see them around anymore.
Ah… but that’s worst case scenario.
Yes. It should be fine. I mean, look outside--

BIRDS CHIRPING

It’s a lovely day. Our home will be safe.
And even if something happens, I’ll be able to support us, so don’t worry.
Thanks, Dr. Dad.

We need to 'prepare'?

It’s best to have an escape bag, just in case.
If we never use it, great. But, just in case, I’m going to start putting all our documents together.
What about Grandpa and Grandma? Do they have everything? They’re so old now…
I don’t even think my father has a passport, to be honest. He hasn’t left his town in thirty years!
We’ll see what we can get for them, but for now, we just need to make sure we have things in place. Just in case.

This all sounds like you’re blowing it out of proportion.

Haha, we probably are, champ. Don’t worry too much about it.

Yeah. You’ll be able to live uninterrupted, probably.

Yes, if you can get off your phone.

I’m not on it now!

Good! Stay that way and eat your food!

Wow, okay, haha.

He’s teasing you, honey.

I know, I know.

I had thought that everything was going to be fine forever. Mom and Dad weren’t worried, after all. Why would I have cared about diplomatic tensions or whatever? It wasn’t my job. I was just thinking about my classes and what I’m going to study in university. I have to go, after all- Mom and Dad both are way too smart to not let me. Other than that, though, there wasn’t anything to worry about.

FADE TO BLACK

Interlude

I remember watching the news. It… I didn’t know how to feel. It felt too far away, and yet, here it was, happening right in front of me.
