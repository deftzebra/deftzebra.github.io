Game Mechanics

In each level, the player cannot progress until they have completed the following:

  • Interacted with all of the interactable elements
  • Looked at all of the artifacts
  • Spoken to every interactable character in the room. This unlocks one additional character for interaction. The player returns to speak to this character, triggering the cutscene.

Each interaction works as a flashback memory and certain interactions give the player solid memories.

  • These memories are collected as items in the inventory - 10 items to be collected before moving to the next level.
  • A memory can be a picture, a story, or an item

During cutscenes, the player can make choices that determine whether the scene progresses, but does not determine the outcome of the scene

  • Wrong choice: The scene does not progress (circular)
  • Right choice: The remainder of the scene plays out and the player advances to the next level

Choices in interactions impact player’s points on one scale: resilience

Script will indicate whether the option will build or take away from resilience

  • 1 option is more self-centered and anxious
  • 1 option is calmer and more empathetic

Player’s stats will track 3 measures: Stress, Resilience, Items

Stress measures the state of the environment - is always 100%

  • Indicates the challenges that the character is going through at the time

    • Resilience measures the state of the character, based on their choices - Goal is to get 100%

    • need to gain sufficient resilience to meet the amount of stress in that situation
    • Player will be able to view these choices’ impacts on their stat, which will inform them of how the character is changing from these events

    Items measures the progress in collecting the memories needed to advance in that level: ie, 8 out of 10

    Control scheme, that includes mouse, keyboard and motion controllers, is efficient and familiar to the user. These mainstream input devices allow the player to focus on the game and lead to an immersive experience.

    • mouse – rotates the character
    • keyboard – initiates forward and sideways movement; hotkeys – shortcuts to some of the character’s movements
